Marin Voice: Queer moms instilled a superpower of LGBTQ+ advocacy

June 5, 2025 | Marin Independent Journal

In 1990s San Francisco, my mothers joined a small but mighty group of queer women making history by embarking on the journey of motherhood. Nearly three decades later, I recognize that my upbringing was a gift, maybe even my superpower.

Growing up with two moms in San Francisco taught me early that love takes many forms, and that the people you love are the ones you choose. My mothers were fierce advocates not just for their own family, but for LGBTQ+ rights and visibility at a time when that was genuinely risky. They showed me that living authentically and fighting for justice aren't separate things—they're intertwined.

This piece is an abridged version of my foreword for Boyhood Reimagined: Stories of Queer Moms Raising Sons, an anthology released in June 2025 highlighting the experiences and ambitions of lesbian mothers—including my own family.
