Snippets From My Year in Rwanda

September 10, 2022 | Shooting Touch Fellowship

I still haven't quite wrapped my head around it all—leaving Rwanda, working at BWB, traveling, returning home—but at the moment, I just feel immense gratitude for the chance to explore the world, meet some of NBA's top coaches and executives, and to engage with the next generation of African basketball stars.

My year in Rwanda through the Shooting Touch fellowship was transformative. I arrived thinking I knew what I was coming to do, but I left having learned infinitely more than I could have imagined.

The people I met, from the women on the courts to the coaches and community leaders, showed me what resilience, determination, and joy look like. Basketball became the vehicle for so much more—for conversations about opportunity, for building relationships across difference, for creating spaces where young people could imagine futures for themselves.

I'm grateful to everyone who was part of this journey, and I can't wait to see where the work of Shooting Touch continues.

Whirlwind

June 24, 2022 | Shooting Touch Fellowship

In a given week, you'll find me driving around Rwanda in an old broken-down Rav4 that was imported from Ottawa, meeting fascinating people from all over the world, organizing Shooting Touch programs, guiding guests around the country, exploring natural wonders, and hooping every chance I get.

The rhythm of life here is different from what I'm used to. Time moves differently. Days are full—sometimes chaotically full—with unexpected conversations, opportunities, and adventures.

One day I'm at a basketball court in a rural village, the next I'm helping coordinate a coaching clinic. One week I'm hiking through coffee plantations, the next I'm facilitating conversations between international coaches and local youth leaders.

What strikes me most is how connected everything feels here. The basketball community in Kigali is tight-knit, and everyone seems to know everyone. It creates this beautiful web of relationships that makes the work possible.

Even on the hard days—and there are hard days—I'm struck by the privilege of being here and the responsibility that comes with it.

This Isn't About Me

March 24, 2022 | Shooting Touch Fellowship

This isn't about me. Or any of the Shooting Touch Rwanda Fellows, or even Shooting Touch. It's about the women who have to strategize every day in order to survive, keep their children healthy, manage their homes, businesses, crops, or livestock, and play basketball with their friends when the work is done.

I came to Rwanda with a certain narrative in my head about what I would do here, what impact I would have. But being here has fundamentally shifted my perspective.

The women I've met—on the courts and off—are the ones doing the real work. They're balancing impossible responsibilities with grace and humor. They're showing up for each other. They're creating community in contexts that would exhaust most of us.

My role here isn't to save anyone or to be the hero of this story. It's to show up with genuine curiosity, to listen, to support the work that's already happening, and to learn from the wisdom that's already in this community.

When I go home, the work will continue here. And that's exactly how it should be.

Change of Pace

February 11, 2022 | Shooting Touch Fellowship

When I arrived in Kigali on January 10, basketball was still banned, and Shooting Touch courts were empty. I had no idea when the regulations might shift in our favor, and I was unsure how my fellowship would proceed without the ability to play the beautiful game. Then, miraculously, our whole situation changed.

The uncertainty was real. I had come prepared to work with basketball and the community around it, but suddenly that wasn't an option. I had to sit with that discomfort and figure out what to do next.

But the Shooting Touch team here in Kigali didn't waste time. They pivoted. They stayed connected to the community. And when the ban lifted, we were ready to jump back in—stronger, more connected, and with even clearer purpose.

That experience taught me something important: resilience isn't just about pushing through difficulties. It's about flexibility, creativity, and staying committed to your mission even when the path isn't clear.

We're back on the courts now, and the energy is incredible. Every practice feels like a gift.

It's All Coming Together

December 30, 2021 | Shooting Touch Fellowship

Arriving in Rwanda just three weeks ago, I can already feel the momentum building. The Shooting Touch team here—both the expatriate and Rwandan staff—are energized and ready to expand programs in 2022.

Over the past few weeks, I've met with partners, visited courts in Kigali and surrounding areas, and started to understand the landscape of sport-for-development work in Rwanda. What strikes me most is the dedication of everyone involved—from the coaches on the courts to the leadership at headquarters.

Basketball is more than a game here. It's a vehicle for connection, for opportunity, for change. And I'm honored to be part of this work.
