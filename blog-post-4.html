This Isn't About Me

March 24, 2022 | Shooting Touch

This isn't about me. Or any of the Shooting Touch Rwanda Fellows, or even Shooting Touch. It's about the women who have to strategize every day in order to survive, keep their children healthy, manage their homes, businesses, crops, or livestock, and play basketball with their friends when the work is done.

I came to Rwanda with a certain narrative in my head about what I would do here, what impact I would have. But being here has fundamentally shifted my perspective.

The women I've met—on the courts and off—are the ones doing the real work. They're balancing impossible responsibilities with grace and humor. They're showing up for each other. They're creating community in contexts that would exhaust most of us.

My role here isn't to save anyone or to be the hero of this story. It's to show up with genuine curiosity, to listen, to support the work that's already happening, and to learn from the wisdom that's already in this community.

When I go home, the work will continue here. And that's exactly how it should be.
